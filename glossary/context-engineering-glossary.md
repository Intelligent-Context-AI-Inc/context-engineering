# Context Engineering Glossary

A reference glossary of terms used in the context engineering discipline and Enterprise Context Fabric architecture.

---

**Context Assembly**
The process of combining normalized signals from multiple enterprise systems into structured context objects suitable for AI consumption.

**Context Assembly Layer**
The middle layer of Enterprise Context Fabric architecture responsible for executing assembly patterns, ranking signals, and producing Context Capsules.

**Context Capsule**
A pre-assembled, structured package of contextual information designed to be delivered to AI systems. Includes metadata, structured context, and governance tags.

**Context Delivery**
The process of transmitting assembled context from Enterprise Context Fabric to AI models, agents, and applications.

**Context Delivery Layer**
The outermost layer of Enterprise Context Fabric architecture responsible for format adaptation, delivery optimization, and governance enforcement at the point of consumption.

**Context Engineering**
The discipline of designing how contextual information is gathered, structured, ranked, governed, and delivered to AI systems.

**Context Governance**
The application of access controls, compliance rules, data classification, and audit trails to context throughout its lifecycle.

**Context Ingestion**
The process of connecting to enterprise source systems and extracting raw signals for context assembly. The first layer of Enterprise Context Fabric architecture.

**Context Ranking**
The process of prioritizing signals based on relevance, recency, and importance to a specific task or query.

**ContextECF**
An implementation of Enterprise Context Fabric architecture developed by Intelligent Context AI, Inc. A production platform for enterprise context assembly and delivery.

**Deterministic Context Assembly**
The practice of assembling context using pre-defined, repeatable patterns rather than probabilistic retrieval methods. Ensures predictability, traceability, and governance by design.

**Enterprise AI Memory**
Persistent, structured storage of contextual information that AI systems can access across sessions, tasks, and workflows within an organization.

**Enterprise Context Fabric**
An architectural pattern for connecting, assembling, and delivering contextual information from enterprise systems to AI models and agents.

**Freshness Window**
The time period during which assembled context is considered current and valid for delivery without reassembly.

**Normalized Signal**
A raw data element extracted from an enterprise system and converted into a standardized format for context assembly.

**Signal**
A discrete piece of data or information extracted from an enterprise system during context ingestion.

**Signal Extraction**
The process of identifying and pulling relevant data from source systems based on defined extraction rules.

**Signal Fusion**
The process of combining multiple signals from different sources into a coherent, structured context object.

**Source System**
An enterprise platform from which signals are extracted during context ingestion (e.g., Salesforce, Slack, Jira, GitHub, ServiceNow).

**Structured Context**
Organized, ranked information assembled from raw signals and ready for AI consumption.

**Time-to-Context**
A performance metric measuring the elapsed time required to gather, assemble, and deliver contextual information needed for AI reasoning or decision making.
