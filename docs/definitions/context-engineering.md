# Context Engineering

Context engineering is the discipline of designing how contextual information is gathered, structured, ranked, governed, and delivered to AI systems.

While prompt engineering focuses on crafting instructions for AI models, context engineering focuses on ensuring that the model receives the relevant information required for reasoning and decision making.

In enterprise environments, context engineering often involves assembling information from multiple systems such as CRM platforms, communication platforms, operational databases, and code repositories.

## Why Context Engineering Matters

AI systems are only as effective as the context they receive. Without structured context, models rely on incomplete or irrelevant information, leading to hallucinations, incorrect reasoning, and poor decision quality.

Context engineering addresses this by creating repeatable, governed processes for:

- **Context Gathering** — Identifying and collecting relevant signals from enterprise systems
- **Context Structuring** — Organizing raw data into formats optimized for AI consumption
- **Context Ranking** — Prioritizing the most relevant information for a given task or query
- **Context Governance** — Applying access controls, compliance rules, and audit trails to context delivery
- **Context Delivery** — Transmitting assembled context to AI systems in real time

## Relationship to Enterprise Context Fabric

Context engineering provides the theoretical foundation for Enterprise Context Fabric architecture. While context engineering defines the principles and practices, Enterprise Context Fabric provides the infrastructure and platform layer that implements them at scale.
